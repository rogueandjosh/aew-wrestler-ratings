About the Ratings

Wrestling is all about feelings, emotions and opinions. It will never be possible to completely accurately compare wrestlers' current form - there are so many factors to consider. However, this system takes account of multiple factors so that wrestlers can be directly compared and tracked over time. It equates them to a numerical value where '100' is average.

The ratings are based on match performances from the excellent Cagematch.net site - not wins and losses, but how fans have received and rated each AEW wrestler's match.

Key Points:

  • All wrestlers start at 100 - points gained/lost based on Cagematch match ratings and votes
  • Factors include: current rating, opponent quality, show type, and match stakes
  • Five divisions: Men's, Women's, Men's Tag, Women's Tag, and Trios (crossover allowed)
  • Minimum 2 matches required to appear in rankings
  • Ratings degrade over time toward 100 - wrestlers drop out when they reach 100
